Jelly Basics

Jellyfish are part of the phylum cnidaria, which includes sea anemonies and coral. Animals that are part of the phylum as soft bodied and can sting as a form of self defense or when capturing their prey.
Cnidarians are mainly found in aquatic environments. Jellyfish are made up of three layers: the epidermis (outer layer), mesoglea (middle layer made out of a thick jelly like substance), and the gastrodermis (inner layer). Jellyfish have no coelom and therefore no cephalisation, meaning the have no brain, blood or even hearts. A coelom is a fluid-dilled body cavity inside of the animal, it allows for growth which in turn allows for the formation of internal organs.



Fun Facts about Jellies

  • Jellyfish are IMMORTAL! The immortal jellyfish (Turritopsis dohrnii) can revert from an adult jelly back into a small polyp
  • Jellyfish have a through-gut, meaning they poop and eat out of the same orfice
  • A group of jellyfish is called a bloom (so pretty!!!)
  • Jellyfish are old! While they have no bones, they do leave behind fossil (which are extremely rare) and scientist believe they've been around for at least 500 million years, possibly more
